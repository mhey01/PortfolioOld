A Little Bit About Me

Mechanical Engineering MEng, Prospective Front-End Developer, Soon-to-be Keyboard Wizard

I graduated as a Master of Engineering from Queen's University Belfast on July 3rd 2019 with an Upper 2.1 Honours. Through my time at University, I found a deep passion for coding and I have decided to pursue a career following that interest. I was first drawn to Engineering as a discipline due its nature in combining creativity and logic to solve problems and create amazing things. Then I discovered coding. I had just found a tool with almost limitless potential to produce crazy and wonderful applications for almost anything. It's almost like being a wizard, but with a keyboard in place of a staff (I'm a bit of a nerd).

But, I was still an engineer. My degree included a placement year where I went away and worked as a full time engineer. And I really didn't like it. At all. I still kept my programming skills with me and used them wherever I could - I created a Python script that would scan complicated technical drawings to highlight symbols relating to components in the field that needed to be investigated - but it became clear to me that I wasn't meant to be an engineer. At least not in this sense of the word.

Nevertheless, I persisted and got the job done. When my placement came to an end, I decided that I would alter my career path. As a visual person, I love seeing code turn into beautiful and creative things on the screen, so I dove head-first into learning web development. I purchased a course on Udemy by Jonas Schmedtmann titled 'Build Responsive Real World Websites with HTML5 and CSS3'. I loved every moment, and when I finished it I wanted more.

But by this time, I was returning to University to complete my final year, and I knew I wouldn't have the time to get my hands dirty with web development for a while. I worked hard and specialised my degree with Computational Engineering modules and a Master's thesis involving programming and simulation. Besides the stress that comes with a student's final year at University, I loved my time at QUB and I am now working with them to hopefully publish two articles based on my thesis in academic journals.

I have now turned my focus solely to becoming programmer (or a keyboard wizard). Blessed with free time, I got myself back on Udemy with 'The Complete JavaScript Course' by Jonas Schmedtmann and I am working my way through. I'm working on projects every day, and everything I make I am getting one step closer to my goal of becoming a developer.

Thanks for taking the time to read this. Have a great day.

Matt
